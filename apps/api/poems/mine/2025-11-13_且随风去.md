# 且随风去

有一天，
我想要和你，
将阻隔我们的山水踏遍，
去最远的天涯浪迹。
背起行囊，只装，
一袭蓑衣，一缕轻烟，
一支笙箫，一片诗意。

有多久，
我们没有，
好好去闻，百花的芬芳，
好好去看，日落的绚烂，
好好去听，海浪的声音，
好好去品，香茗的滋味，
好好去触，土地的温度，
好好去读，古卷的气韵。

有多美，
青翠欲滴的绿草如茵，
璀璨夺目的霞光万丈，
荡魂摄魄的鲸落万物生。
如此万紫千红的世间，
如此绮丽旖旎的风光，
如何可错过？

何其幸，
我将此心照明月，
明月对我亦如是，
既可遁入红尘嚣嚣，
也可飘然超凡出世。

任我行，
与你随风去，
只如初见，
不道是寻常。

## Version A — Literal (faithful to wording over flow)

One day,
I want, with you,
to tread across all the mountains and waters that stand between us,
to wander to the farthest edge of the earth.
Shouldering our packs, and packing only
a coir rain-cloak, a wisp of light smoke,
a bamboo flute, a fragment of poetic feeling.

How long
has it been since we
truly smelled the fragrance of a hundred flowers,
truly watched the splendor of the sunset,
truly listened to the sound of the sea’s waves,
truly tasted the savor of fine tea,
truly touched the warmth of the soil,
truly read the spirit and rhythm of ancient scrolls.

How beautiful—
green grass lush as a carpet, dripping with verdure;
ten-thousand-ray brilliance of glowing clouds;
the soul-seizing whale-fall from which all things are born.
Such a world of myriad colors,
such gorgeous and delicate scenery—
how could it be missed?

How fortunate—
I shine this heart to the bright moon;
the bright moon treats me the same.
We can hide within the clamor of the dusty world,
or drift lightly, transcending the mortal realm.

Let me roam,
go with you on the wind,
just as at first meeting,
and call it nothing out of the ordinary.



## Version B — Liberal (keeps the poem’s structure)

One day,
I want to walk with you
over every river and mountain that once kept us apart,
to drift toward the farthest rim of the world.
We shoulder our packs, carrying only
a straw rain-cloak, a curl of smoke,
a flute’s thin breath, a pocket of poetry.

How long
has it been since we
really breathed the scent of a hundred blooms,
really watched the sunset blaze,
really heard the speaking sea,
really tasted what good tea says,
really felt the earth give off its warmth,
really read the pulse of ancient pages.

How beautiful—
grass so green it gleams like velvet;
sky-fire flung in ten-thousand beams;
a whale’s last fall, soul-still, feeding life.
Such riotous color in this world,
such lavish, lissome views—
how could we pass them by?

What luck—
I hold my heart up to the moon,
and the moon answers in kind.
We can vanish into the city’s dust,
or rise, unburdened, beyond the world.

Let me go,
wind-borne beside you,
as it was when we first met,
and let it pass for ordinary.
